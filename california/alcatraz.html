    Alcatraz Island

    prison in past - museum today

    Info

    Alcatraz Island is the small island was developed with facilities for a lighthouse, a military fortification, a military prison from 1868, and a federal prison from 1933 until 1963. In 1972, Alcatraz became a national recreation area and received designation as a National Historic Landmark in 1986.

    The island became a Federal Bureau of Prisons federal prison in August 1934. It was designed to hold prisoners who continuously caused trouble at other federal prisons. Most of the prisoners were notorious bank robbers and murderers. During the 29 years it was in use, the jail held some of the most notorious criminals in American history, such as Al Capone, Robert Franklin Stroud (the Birdman of Alcatraz), George "Machine Gun" Kelly. The penitentiary claimed that no prisoner successfully escaped.

    The entire Alcatraz Island was listed on the National Register of Historic Places in 1976, and was further declared a National Historic Landmark in 1986.

    ※

    In 1775, the island was identified as the "La Isla de los Alcatraces", which translates from the archaic Spanish as "The Island of the Pelicans". Over the years, the English version "Alcatraz" became popular and is now widely used.
