    Golden Gate

    a graceful suspension bridge

    Info

    Although the idea of a bridge spanning the Golden Gate strait was not new, the proposal that eventually took hold was made in a 1916 San Francisco Bulletin article by former engineering student James Wilkins. The bridge was only finished and opened May 27, 1937. The structure links the U.S. city of San Francisco, on the northern tip of the San Francisco Peninsula, to Marin County.

    The Frommers travel guide considers the Golden Gate "possibly the most beautiful, certainly the most photographed, bridge in the world". The bridge is one of the most internationally recognized symbols of San Francisco, California, and the United States. It has been declared one of the Wonders of the Modern World by the American Society of Civil Engineers.
