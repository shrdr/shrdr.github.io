    Cable Cars

    An icon of San Francisco

    Info

    The first cable car line started regular service on September 1, 1873, and its success led it to become the template for other cable car transit systems.

    While the cable cars are used to a certain extent by commuters, the vast majority of their 7 million annual passengers are tourists.

    The cable cars, running at a constant speed of 15.3 km/h, are pulled by a cable running below the street. The cables are coated with a tar-like material which serves as a sacrificial lubricant (much like a pencil eraser erodes away rather than the paper).

    ※

    The San Francisco Cable Car system is the last working system of its kind in the world.
